Cyber Crime Helpline

If you are a victim of cyber crime, do not panic. Report it immediately using the official helpline. Early reporting increases the chance of recovery and legal action against the attacker.

📞 National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930

Online Portal: cybercrime.gov.in — Available 24 hours, 7 days a week

Helpline Services

Cyber helplines provide assistance to victims of cyber crimes by helping them report incidents and take action. They guide users in recovering accounts and preventing further damage.

What the Helpline Can Help You With:
  • Online financial fraud and UPI scams
  • Account hacking and unauthorized access
  • Cyberbullying and online harassment
  • Identity theft and misuse of personal data
  • Phishing and fake website complaints

When you call 1930 or visit cybercrime.gov.in, have your screenshots, transaction IDs, and account details ready. The faster you report, the higher the chance of freezing fraudulent transactions.

Steps to Take After a Cyber Attack

If you suspect you have been attacked, act quickly. Every minute counts — especially in cases of financial fraud where transactions can still be reversed if reported fast enough.

  1. Disconnect from the internet immediately to stop any ongoing data theft or unauthorized access.
  2. Change all passwords on compromised accounts from a different, safe device.
  3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all your important accounts right away.
  4. Call your bank immediately if any financial fraud has occurred. Request a freeze on suspicious transactions.
  5. Take screenshots of all suspicious messages, emails, or transactions as evidence before deleting anything.
  6. File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930 with all collected evidence.
  7. Run a full antivirus scan on your device to detect and remove any installed malware.
⚠ Important: Do not format your device or delete any messages before filing the complaint. Evidence is critical for police investigation.
Types of Cyber Crimes You Can Report

Cyber crime covers a wide range of offenses. Here are the main categories that can be reported through official channels in India:

  • Online Financial Fraud: Unauthorized bank transfers, UPI fraud, credit/debit card misuse, lottery scams.
  • Cyberbullying & Harassment: Threatening messages, defamation, stalking, and online abuse on social media.
  • Identity Theft: Someone using your name, photos, or personal documents to create fake profiles or commit fraud.
  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to your email, social media, or computer systems.
  • Phishing & Fake Websites: Being tricked into entering credentials on a fake banking or shopping site.
  • Child Safety Crimes: Any inappropriate content involving minors should be reported immediately to the cyber cell.

Global Cyber Security Organizations

Several national and international organizations work to combat cyber crime and protect digital infrastructure around the world.

  • CERT-In (India): The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team handles national cyber security incidents and issues advisories. Website: cert-in.org.in
  • CISA (USA): The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency protects critical US infrastructure from cyber threats.
  • Interpol Cyber Unit: Coordinates global cybercrime investigations across member countries.
  • ENISA (Europe): The EU Agency for Cybersecurity provides guidance and support to European member states.
  • NCSC (UK): The National Cyber Security Centre helps UK businesses and citizens defend against cyber threats.
